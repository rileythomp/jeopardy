Jeopardy Info

It seems like this is your first time playing! This is a multiplayer Jeopardy game. It should be familiar to anyone who has seen it on TV, but there are a few differences to make it playable online. Please take a moment to carefully ready the info below, paying close attention to the bolded sections. If you ever need to refer to this info again, you can click the icon in the top right corner of the page.

Picking: Players have 30 seconds to pick a question. If no question is picked, the lowest value question will be chosen.

Buzzing: Players have 30 seconds to read the question and buzz in. If all 3 players pass, the question will be skipped.

Answering: Players have 30 seconds to type in an answer after buzzing in. Do not phrase your answer in the form of a question like on TV. For example, answer "This city is the capital of Germany" with "Berlin" rather than "What is Berlin?"

Voting: We cannot guarantee that the computer will always judge an answer correctly. So after each answer, players have 10 seconds to challenge the computer's judgement. If 2/3 players challenge the computer's judgement, then it is overturned and the player's score will be adjusted accordingly.

Wagering: Players have 30 seconds to wager during Double and Final Jeopardy. If no wager is given, the lowest allowed wager will be chosen.

Note: This game is still in development. If you encounter any issues or have any suggestions, please file an issue!
